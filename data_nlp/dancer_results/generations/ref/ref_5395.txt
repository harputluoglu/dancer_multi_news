Judge Vance Day—former chairman of the state's Republican Party—created a legal defense fund yesterday, apparently to pay for expenses he expects to incur fighting allegations he's violating the state's Code of Judicial Conduct as well as its constitution, reports Oregon Live “It’s an exercise of his religious freedom rights under the First Amendment,” his spokesperson tells KGW In Oregon, public officials are allowed to set up trusts to pay for legal defenses against government investigations, Oregon Live reports.
